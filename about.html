About me

Engineer, operator, problem-solver.

I’m Mayibongwinkosi Jordan Ndlovu, an AWS Infrastructure Engineer based in Johannesburg, South Africa. I work at the intersection of cloud infrastructure, systems administration, support operations and access management — helping keep environments stable, secure and usable for the people who depend on them every day.

Who I am

A little about my journey

My path into tech wasn’t a straight line. I started in infrastructure and networking support, spent time working in fibre deployment and network operations, and gradually moved deeper into cloud engineering. That mix of hands-on support, systems administration and infrastructure work shaped the way I approach technology today: I care about what works in production, what breaks under pressure, and how to make systems easier to support.

Over the last few years, I’ve built experience across AWS infrastructure, enterprise support, authentication and access administration, incident troubleshooting, and operational support. I’m currently working as an AWS Engineer at Marquis Finance, where I support and troubleshoot cloud environments, manage access and authentication issues, assist with SFTP environments, and help keep core systems available and reliable.

Before that, I worked in cloud and DevOps support roles where I was exposed to Jenkins, GitHub workflows, Terraform, Docker, AWS deployments and infrastructure operations. Earlier in my career, I worked in networking and systems administration roles, supporting Windows Server environments, Office 365, DNS, DHCP, Active Directory and general IT operations.

How I work

My approach to engineering

I’m drawn to the kind of work that sits behind the scenes but matters when it counts: fixing access issues before they become blockers, tightening cloud configurations, restoring service when something breaks, and putting processes in place so the same issue doesn’t happen twice. I enjoy solving practical problems — the kind that affect users, teams and delivery in real time.

A lot of my work revolves around reliability, supportability and clarity. I like systems that are well-documented, repeatable and secure. I also like being the person who can step into a messy issue, break it down calmly, and get it moving again.

My core stack is AWS-focused, but I’m equally comfortable in the overlap between cloud, infrastructure, support and operations. That includes services like EC2, S3, IAM, VPC, Lambda, Route 53, CloudWatch and AWS Transfer Family, as well as the day-to-day operational side of support: MFA recovery, user access, troubleshooting, service monitoring and technical documentation.

What I’m building toward

Where I’m headed

Right now, I’m focused on growing further as a cloud and infrastructure engineer — especially in roles that combine AWS, automation, platform operations, systems reliability and modern support engineering. I’m interested in environments where I can keep deepening my technical skills while still staying close to real operational work and business impact.

I’m also completing further studies in Information Technology, because I want my practical engineering experience and academic growth to reinforce each other over time. Long term, I want to keep building a career around cloud infrastructure, operational excellence and the kind of engineering work that quietly keeps everything running.

Beyond work

Outside the job title

Outside of work, I’m always trying to sharpen the next layer of my skill set — whether that’s learning a new AWS service, improving how I document and structure my work, or refining the way I present projects and experience. I enjoy technology that is practical, useful and well put together, and I tend to bring that same mindset into the way I build things for myself as well.

If you’re here because of cloud infrastructure, support engineering, AWS operations, or just because you like well-built systems and clean problem-solving, you’re in the right place.
